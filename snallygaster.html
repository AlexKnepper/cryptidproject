The Snallygaster

Description

The snallygaster is said to resemble a dragon-like creature, or is sometimes depicted as having features similar to a bird or bat, but incredibly warped. It’s main quirks are its long tentacles, most often depicted as coming from its beak or around its mouth, sharp claws and teeth, massive wings, and a large, yellow eye resting universally in the middle of its forehead.

History/Origin

The snallygaster comes from stories told by German settlers in the early 1700s. The creature gets its name from the German name “schneller geist” which literally means “quick spirit”. The Snallygaster made a re-appearance in the 19th century, although many postulate that the re-emergence of the legend was circulated with the idea of frightening runaway slaves.
