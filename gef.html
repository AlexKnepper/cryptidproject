Gef The Mongoose

Description

Gef the Mongoose was, quite obviously, a mongoose; albeit one with the supposed ability of human speech. Gef was described as being “the size of a small rat with yellowish fur and a large bushy tail.” Despite this description, the creature continually made claims of being a terrifying spectre, which ran much contrary to the family’s pet-like treatment of Gef, such as taking him to the market.

History/Origin

Gef the Mongoose, also known as the Dalby Spook, was claimed to be discovered by the Irving Family in Britain in the early 1930s. They reported a persistent scratching of something small behind their walls, akin to a ferret, dog, or child. Not long after this scratching, Gef introduced itself as “a ghost in the form of a mongoose” born in New Dehli, India in 1852.
